Gizdodo

Description

Gizdodo is a flavorful Nigerian dish made by combining fried plantains and spicy peppered chicken gizzards in a rich tomato stew. Loved for its sweet and spicy balance, it's served with rice or enjoyed as a party starter, making it a favorite in many Nigerian homes.

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe plantains
  • 450 grams gizzard
  • 4 medium size tomatoes
  • 2 red bell pepper
  • 1 whole onion 1/2 onion chopped
  • 1/2 red, green, yellow bell pepper each chopped
  • 1.5 scotch bonnet
  • Vegetable/sunflower oil
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 tsp chicken seasoning ( I use Aromat)
  • 1/2 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare the Plantains: Peel and cut the plantains into bite-sized pieces. Fry in hot oil until golden brown and set aside.
  2. Cook the Gizzards: Boil the gizzards until tender, then cut into bite-sized pieces. Fry lightly in oil for extra flavor and set aside.
  3. Blend the Tomato-Pepper Base: Blend the tomatoes, 2 red bell peppers, 1 whole onion, and scotch bonnets until smooth.
  4. Fry the Sauce: Heat oil in a skillet, pour in the blended mixture, and cook on medium heat until the sauce thickens and oil separates. Add curry powder, garlic powder, thyme, chicken seasoning, and salt.
  5. Combine Ingredients: Add the fried gizzards, fried plantains, and chopped bell peppers and ½ onion. Stir gently to coat everything in the spicy sauce.
  6. Simmer and Serve: Reduce heat, allow flavors to meld for 5 - 10 minutes, then serve hot. Perfect as a rice accompaniment or as a party starter.
