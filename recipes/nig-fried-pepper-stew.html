Nigerian Fried Pepper Stew

Description

Nigerian Fried Pepper Stew (Ata Dindin)** is a rich, spicy Nigerian stew made from blended peppers, tomatoes, onions, and assorted meats, fried in oil until deeply flavorful. This version offers an easy, delicious method perfect for pairing with rice or other staples.

Ingredients

  • 4 Red Bell Peppers (Tatashe)
  • 500g Assorted meats Boiled Eggs (Optional)
  • 3/4 cup Palm Oil
  • 3/4 Cup Vegetable Oil
  • 1.5 Whole Onions
  • Scotch bonnet (Ata rodo)
  • 1/2 Cup Dried Prawns
  • 1/2 Chopped Onion
  • 4 Tablespoons Crayfish Powder
  • Chicken/ Beef Seasoning
  • 3 Tablespoons Locust Beans
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Blend the Peppers: Combine the red bell peppers, scotch bonnets, and one onion. Blend until smooth and set aside.
  2. Heat the Oils: In a large pan, heat both palm oil and vegetable oil together until hot but not smoky.
  3. Fry the Locust Beans and Onions: Add the chopped half onion and locust beans. Saut√© for 2 - 3 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add the Pepper Blend: Pour in the blended pepper mix and cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and the oil begins to separate from the stew.
  5. Add Seasonings and Proteins: Stir in the crayfish powder, dried prawns, seasoning cubes, and salt. Add the pre-boiled assorted meats (and boiled eggs if using). Cook for 10 - 15 minutes to allow flavors to combine.
  6. Simmer and Serve: Reduce the heat and let the stew simmer until rich and well-fried. Serve hot with rice, yam, or plantains.
